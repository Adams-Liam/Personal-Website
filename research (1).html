Research

My work centers on cognitive psychology and theory within Extended Reality and AI — aiming to strengthen the foundational principles of how we design and understand human-computer interaction.

Ongoing
Current Project

Peeking in 3D: Mixed Reality and the Cost of Cognitive Offloading

This project investigates how mixed reality environments influence cognitive offloading strategies. As users increasingly rely on digital augmentation to manage cognitive tasks, this work examines the associated costs and trade-offs from a cognitive psychology perspective.

ReadAR: Utilizing Mixed Reality for Physical Document Digitization

ReadAR explored the use of mixed reality as a tool for digitizing physical documents. The project examined how augmented interfaces can bridge the gap between tangible paper-based information and digital workflows.
